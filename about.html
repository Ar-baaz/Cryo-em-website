About the Company

Moleverse is an innovative startup company operating in the field of cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM), offering cutting-edge services in structural biology. With expertise in this advanced imaging technique, Moleverse provides solutions for visualizing and analyzing the structures of biological molecules at near-atomic resolution.

Utilizing cryo-EM, Moleverse enables researchers and scientists to examine the intricate details of proteins, nucleic acids, and other macromolecular complexes. This technology allows for the visualization of biological samples in their native state, without the need for extensive sample preparation or staining. By capturing high-resolution images of these molecules, Moleverse aids in understanding their three-dimensional structures, leading to insights into their functions and potential applications.

Moleverse's team of experts works closely with clients from various sectors, including academic institutions, pharmaceutical companies, and biotechnology firms. They collaborate on projects ranging from basic research to drug discovery and development. Through their comprehensive cryo-EM services, Moleverse supports clients in accelerating their scientific advancements and translating them into real-world applications.

By leveraging state-of-the-art cryo-EM instruments and advanced computational tools, Moleverse delivers accurate and reliable structural biology data. Their services encompass sample preparation, data collection, image processing, and structural analysis. Whether clients require single-particle analysis, tomography, or other cryo-EM techniques, Moleverse provides tailored solutions to meet their specific needs.

Moleverse is committed to staying at the forefront of cryo-EM technology and continuously investing in research and development. By pushing the boundaries of structural biology, they contribute to scientific discoveries and pave the way for new breakthroughs in fields such as drug design, molecular biology, and bioengineering.

What we do.

2D Class Averaging

2D averaging is a computational method used to improve the clarity and resolution of a molecule's structure. It involves aligning and averaging multiple 2D images obtained through electron microscopy or imaging techniques. By reducing noise and artifacts, this technique enhances the visualization of the molecule's shape and organization, leading to a more accurate understanding of its structure.

3d structure reconstruction

3D structure reconstruction is a technique used to determine the three-dimensional arrangement of molecules, such as proteins or nucleic acids. It involves combining data from various sources, such as X-ray crystallography, electron microscopy, or nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy, to create a detailed model of the molecule's shape and atomic positions.

Feasibiility Assesment

Feasibility assessment in structural biology involves evaluating the practicality and likelihood of obtaining structural information for a biomolecule. Factors such as available techniques, sample quality, expertise, and potential challenges are considered to determine the viability of the study and guide the selection of the most suitable approach.

Negative Stain Screening

Negative stain screening is a technique in structural biology that uses electron microscopy to quickly assess the shape and structure of macromolecules. By contrasting the molecules with a heavy metal stain, researchers can rapidly screen and identify potential candidates for further detailed analysis, helping in the selection of suitable samples for more extensive studies.

Single Particle Analysis

Single particle analysis is a powerful technique in structural biology that utilizes electron microscopy to determine the three-dimensional structure of macromolecules. By imaging individual particles and computationally analyzing their projection images, researchers can generate detailed 3D reconstructions, enabling the study of molecular structure, function, and organization.
